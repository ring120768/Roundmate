# Pugsie PA — what it does for Paul

**The one-liner:** Paul finishes a job, ticks one box, and his admin does itself. Pugsie PA is the personal assistant ("PA") that works *while* he works.

## The problem it kills

Paul's brilliant on the glass — but the business quietly leaks money and time off the ladder:

- Jobs done but never invoiced — money lost without him even noticing.
- Unpaid jobs he never gets round to chasing.
- Repeat customers who drift off the round because nobody rebooked them.
- Evenings swallowed by the diary, the spreadsheet, and the "who owes me?" headache.
- Fuel and hours wasted criss-crossing town between far-apart jobs.

## The shift: from evening admin to one tick

**Before Pugsie PA:** clean all day → get home knackered → try to remember who paid, who's due, who needs invoicing → half of it never happens → money and customers leak away.

**With Pugsie PA:** clean the windows → tick **"complete"** → the app instantly invoices the customer (or sends a receipt if they paid), books the next visit, and emails them the confirmation. Done — from the van, on his phone.

## What Paul actually gets

- **Gets paid faster, loses less.** Every finished job is invoiced on the spot, and a clear "who owes me money" list means nothing slips through the cracks. That's real cash he was leaving on the table.
- **His evenings back.** The admin that used to eat his nights now happens in a single tap.
- **Repeat work locked in.** The next visit is booked and confirmed automatically, so customers stay on the round year after year — and repeats are the heart of a window-cleaning income.
- **Less driving, more cleaning.** Smart Rounds groups nearby customers by area, so he fills a day in one postcode instead of zig-zagging across town — less fuel, more jobs done.
- **He looks the part.** Tidy, branded invoices and confirmation emails make a one-man round feel like a proper outfit — customers trust it and pay quicker, with fewer "did you even come?" disputes.
- **It lives in his pocket.** A real app on his phone's home screen, big buttons, built to use up a ladder — no laptop, no paperwork.

## The bottom line

Pugsie PA pays for itself the first time it catches an unpaid job Paul would have forgotten, or saves a repeat customer who'd have drifted away. For the price of a couple of cleans a month, he swaps a drawer full of admin for one tick — and gets paid faster, drives less, and keeps more customers.

> **Finish the job. Tick the box. The rest looks after itself.**
